Welcome!

I’m Gül Oymak—a computer scientist in Zurich, currently pursuing an M.Sc. in Computer Science (majoring in Machine Intelligence) at ETH Zurich. I’m broadly interested in AI, with a focus on AI in finance. Lately I’ve been exploring robust time-series modeling and forecasting, risk/anomaly detection, and the post-training stack for language models (SFT + RL) including data curation, reward modeling, and DPO/PPO workflows. I care about end-to-end reliability—clean datasets, reproducible experiments, disciplined evaluation, and clear cost/latency trade-offs—so my projects often include simple backtesting harnesses, monitoring, and tools that make model behavior easier to understand and operate. I like problems that ask for both good theory and practical tradeoffs, and my goal as a machine learning engineer is to build tools that are understandable, reliable, and actually useful.

Outside work, I’m the friend who cooks too much Mediterranean food, swims whenever the weather cooperates, and knows which second-hand shops have the best vintage finds. Pilates keeps me steady; lake walks make me stay. Originally from Turkey, I’ve lived in Zürich long enough to feel like a local, and I love the quiet rhythm of the trams and the small surprises you notice when you slow down.

If you’d like to contact me or work together on something or share some ideas, you can always reach me out!
